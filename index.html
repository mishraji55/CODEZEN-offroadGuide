The Challenge

Why off-road autonomy requires a new approach

Limited Real-World Data

Collecting labeled off-road datasets is expensive, time-consuming, and dangerous. Real-world scenarios vary drastically across regions.

Complex Terrain Variability

Rocks, vegetation, sand, and mixed terrain create high visual ambiguity. Off-road environments lack the structure of urban settings.

Generalization Gap

Models trained on limited data fail on unseen terrain. Traditional computer vision struggles with natural, unstructured environments.

Autonomous Navigation

Off-road vehicles must understand traversability, obstacle detection, and terrain properties in real-time with high accuracy.

Off-Road vs Urban Autonomy

Aspect Urban Autonomy Off-Road Autonomy
Data Availability Abundant (KITTI, Waymo) Extremely Limited
Environment Structure Structured (roads, lanes) Unstructured, Variable
Collection Risk Lower Very High
Semantic Complexity Moderate Extreme

Network Architecture

Encoder: ResNet-50 backbone pretrained on ImageNet
ASPP Module: Atrous Spatial Pyramid Pooling for multi-scale context
Decoder: Bilinear upsampling with skip connections
Output: Segmentation map (6 terrain classes @ 512×512)

Failure Case Analysis

Understanding model limitations and edge cases

Mixed Terrain

Mixed Terrain Ambiguity

Issue: Bushes overlapping with rocky ground cause misclassification.

Root Cause: Similar texture and color distributions between classes.

Mitigation: Enhanced spatial context through larger receptive fields.

Shadow Effects

Extreme Lighting Conditions

Issue: Deep shadows cause sky/vegetation confusion.

Root Cause: Limited training diversity in extreme lighting scenarios.

Mitigation: Added adversarial augmentation for shadow variations.

Small Objects

Small Object Detection

Issue: Small logs and debris are under-segmented.

Root Cause: Downsampling in encoder loses fine details.

Mitigation: Multi-scale decoder with additional skip connections.

Domain Shift

Sim-to-Real Domain Gap

Issue: Real-world images differ from synthetic rendering.

Root Cause: Photorealism limitations in digital twins.

Mitigation: Style transfer and adversarial domain adaptation.
